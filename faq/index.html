FAQs:


  • Didn't this app get shut down before?

    • Yeah, but we're good now. This version doesn't use username or password. It even got a thumbs-up from Divvy's legal team.

  • Can you tell me about some excellent people who helped make DivvyBrags?

    • Why, sure. Nick Bennett wrote super-slick Python scripts to find distances between Divvy stations. The good folks at the Smart Chicago Collaborative lent us their Google Maps for Businesses license. Drew Huening had the idea for a browser extension. Hunter Owens and Dan Gohlke gave great advice, Ian Dees wrote the very first divvybikes.com scraping tool, and — last but not least — the Divvy team (a.) took the time to review this app and (b.) built a great bikeshare system for Chicago.

  • How does DivvyBrags calculate milage?

    • Distance data comes from the Google Distance Matrix API.

  • This app leaves me feeling totally overwhelmed with joy, what should I do?

    • Donate to The Active Transit Alliance or Streetsblog Chicago.
