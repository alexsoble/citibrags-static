FAQs:

  • Didn't this app get shut down before?

    • Yeah... But we're all good now. Basically, Divvy didn't feel that the old version was secure for users (here's the full story). The new DivvyBrags is a Chrome extension, so it doesn't ask for your username or password at any point. It even got a thumbs-up from Divvy's IT team and legal department. Thanks Divvy!

  • Can you tell me about some excellent people who helped make DivvyBrags?

    • Why, certainly. Nick Bennett wrote super-slick Python scripts to find distances between Divvy stations. The good folks at the Smart Chicago Collaborative lent us their Google Maps for Business license. Drew Huening had the idea for a browser extension. Hunter Owens and Dan Gohlke gave great advice. Ian Dees wrote the first scraping tool for the Divvy website. Last but not least, the Divvy team (a.) took the time to review this app and (b.) built a great bikeshare system for Chicago.

  • How does DivvyBrags work?

    • For each trip, DivvyBrags finds the distance from Station A to Station B as measured by the Google Distance Matrix API (cycling mode). The assumption is that you're using Divvy bikes to travel from station to station. DivvyBrags is actually providing a lower bound of sorts for your milage — if you check a bike out at Station A, bike around for 20 minutes, and then check it back in at Station A, your DivvyBrags milage count doesn't increase.

  • This app leaves me feeling totally overwhelmed with joy, what should I do?

    • Donate to The Active Transit Alliance or Streetsblog Chicago.
